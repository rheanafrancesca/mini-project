Work Experience

Bureau Veritas - Co-op Student

May 2024 - December 2024

During my co-op at Bureau Veritas, I worked with the Preprocessing team which supports other departments by preparing samples for further analyses. For samples requiring organic analysis, I extracted environmental samples into glass vials using a top loading balance to obtain their weights. When scanning in new samples from sample logistics, I examined each test code assigned to a sample and determined what preparatory tasks must be performed. This could involve labelling samples, scooping samples that required overnight drying into tin trays before putting them into the oven, and communicating to other team members about immediate deadlines for certain samples. Using the company’s Laboratory Information Management System, I created worksheets for a specific test, recorded data such as the weights of extracted samples, logged the movement of samples between departments and storage, and entered instrument verification data. Once samples were prepared, I delivered the samples to other departments, ensuring that test deadlines were met.

Virogin Biotech – Research Assistant Co-op

January 2022 - September 2022

In my co-op as a research assistant at Virogin Biotech, I worked with the Pharmacology & Toxicology team where my main responsibilities were related to project management. I communicated between the in vivo and in vitro teams about the latest study updates through e-mails and a shared Google Sheet. I was also responsible for maintaining quality assurance records on our electronic lab notebook hosted on LabArchives, and drafting and reviewing documents such as the study protocol and standard operating procedures. In addition, I developed an Excel file that generated models for tumour growth data in mice. This file was designed as a template that could be applied to various in vivo studies. More recently, models generated from this template were featured in the 2023 publication titled “Prophylactic Vaccination and Intratumoral Boost with HER2-Expressing Oncolytic Herpes Simplex Virus Induces Robust and Persistent Immune Response against HER2-Positive Tumor Cells”.
